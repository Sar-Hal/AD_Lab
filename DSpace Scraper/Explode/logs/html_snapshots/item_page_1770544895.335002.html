Title: Quiz as a teaching and learning method in Community Medicine A report of the second state level IAPSM Quiz in Puducherry
Authors: Behera, Basanta Kumar
Keywords: Community Medicine,
Learning method,
Quiz
Issue Date: Jan-2014
Publisher: KIIT University
Citation: The Health Agenda, Volume 2. Issue 1. January, 2014
Abstract: Second State level IAPSM Quiz was conducted in July 2010 in Puducherry. The main purpose of the quiz was to introduce the scope of the subject to medical students and obtain their feedback on its conduct and utility as a teaching and learning method. Initially twelve teams participated and finally four teams could make it to the final. Most liked round were the buzzer (31%) and the rapid fire round (49%). Overall, 69% students liked the quiz very much, 63% found the quiz to be very informative. Majority (87%) felt that, the quiz was conducted in a fair manner. 76% felt that quiz could be an effective method to teach Community Medicine, and 51% felt that quiz should be conducted once every month. 84% felt that after the quiz, they feel stimulated to read the subject in a better way. The perception of the subject changed in 63% of the students, and 37% felt interested in pursuing this discipline further in their career. Quiz can be used effectively to teach the subject, motivate to prepare better and stimulate learning.
