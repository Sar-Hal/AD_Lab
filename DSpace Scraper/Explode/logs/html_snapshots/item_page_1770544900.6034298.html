Title: A Comparative Study On Employee Retention In Public & Private Sector Banks
Authors: AKRITI, KUMARI
Issue Date: Nov-2023
Publisher: KIIT Deemed to be University
Abstract: Retention and turnover are two sides of a coin in today’s scenario. Retention refers to the extent to which employees feel motivated and happy to walk to work every day. It also talks about the point to which people value and believe in their job. Active retention maps the productivity and performance of the employees. The term turnover, serving as an antonym to the word retention, talks about the state of emotional detachment, separation, retreat or retirement from an activity. Engaged workers are the ones you look forward to seeing every Monday morning. They have likely been identified as high-potential employees and feature prominently in their organization’s succession planning process. Disengaged workers, view their jobs as an exchange of time against money. They live a very monotonous work-life with no passion for creativity and innovation from their side. Actively engaged employees turn into disengaged employees due to a list of factors such as, a lack of career growth or promotion, a perception of salary inequity, job dislike, or distrust in their direct manager and senior management. When the banking sector is compared, the studies reveal that employees are more engaged in the banking sector than in the non banking sector. Yet according to the recent trends a lot of bank employees are disengaged because of a set of factors such as cultural variations, politicking, ineffective leadership skills etc. Research papers and articles have explored various facets of turnover but a host of research gaps have been identified such as limited research has been done in case of banks in India and very little research has been done in terms of remedial actions for the improvement of disengaged employees. The purpose of the research shall be to understand the factors responsible for turnover in employees of private and public banks in Bhubaneswar, Odisha and it also aims to provide suggestive measures to curb turnover based on the findings. The contribution of the research shall be to help organizations accelerate their performance by effective employee retention. The research also aims to provide aid to organizations in identifying symptoms of turnover in employees so that they can work on their policy and make their work environment healthier and happier. Thus, the research procedure shall make an attempt to explore the core concept of disengagement and aims at comparing disengagement levels in the Indian IT sector scenario particularly in case of women IT employees in Bhubaneswar, Odisha. The outcome of the present study has shown that there are various factors that affect the turnover levels in the employees of private and public banks in Bhubaneswar, Odisha such as work environment, leadership, team work, co –workers , policies, compensation and training and development. It was also found that there are no different opinions in case of married and unmarried women regarding turnover. The study has given various suggestions based on the findings to incorporate new methods and policies that shall increase the engagement levels of the employees and accelerate employee retention in the banking sector
