Title: Comparative Study of Workplace Wellness Programs (WWP) in Private and Government Healthcare Sectors in Odisha
Authors: Namita Priya
Keywords: organizational leadership
environmental sustainability
workplace wellness programs
healthcare employees
spiritual fulfillment
well-being
mental resilience
social inclusion
physical health
Odisha
Issue Date: 2025
Publisher: KIIT Deemed to be University
Abstract: This study investigates how wellness initiatives in the workplace might benefit healthcare workers' health and happiness in Odisha, focusing on physical, mental, social, spiritual, and environmental dimensions. Using a Using a mixed-methods strategy, the study examines the effects of these programs across private and government healthcare sectors, offering helpful information on how well they work and where they might be enhanced. The findings reveal that physical well-being is significantly improved through initiatives aimed at reducing chronic disease risks, promoting active lifestyles, and providing access to health resources. Health problems are less common and energy levels are higher among employees who take part in wellness initiatives., and better fitness, reflecting the importance of such interventions in addressing physical health challenges in demanding work environments. Mental well-being, another critical dimension, is enhanced through stress management workshops, counseling services, and mindfulness practices, which help employees build resilience, reduce anxiety, and maintain clarity in their professional roles. Programs addressing mental health concerns also foster a supportive workplace atmosphere, encouraging employees to engage more actively in their tasks. Social well-being is promoted through activities that foster teamwork, collaboration, and inclusivity. Employees involved in team-building exercises, peer support programs, and community engagement initiatives report stronger relationships with colleagues and a greater sense of belonging. Such efforts boost morale, which in turn boosts productivity and employee happiness on the job retention. Spiritual well-being, often neglected in workplace discussions, is found to be an essential component of holistic health. Mindfulness practices, opportunities for reflection, and programs that emphasize purpose-driven work help employees align their personal values with organizational goals, resulting in greater job fulfillment and emotional balance. These initiatives encourage employees to see their contributions as meaningful, v promoting satisfaction and improving overall engagement. Environmental well-being is supported through initiatives aimed at creating safe, sustainable, and health-promoting workplace environments. Programs focusing on sustainability, waste reduction, and awareness of environmental issues contribute to employee satisfaction and demonstrate organizational commitment to broader social responsibilities. Such efforts help the company in the long run and boost its image as an ethical and forward-thinking entity. The comparative analysis between private and government healthcare sectors highlights differences in resource availability, leadership styles, and program implementation. Private institutions often access more funding for all-encompassing wellness initiatives, while government hospitals face challenges related to budget constraints and staff shortages. Despite these differences, both sectors demonstrate the potential by using personalised strategies to enhance the health and happiness of our employees. Research also shows that electronics may help wellness initiatives in the workplace be more effective.. Virtual platforms, mobile applications, and digital tools enable employees to access wellness resources conveniently, increasing participation rates and ensuring inclusivity. These tools provide innovative ways to promote mindfulness, fitness, and community engagement, bridging the gap for employees in remote or hybrid work settings. Furthermore, leaders have a crucial role in the achievement of WWPs. Ethical and empathetic leadership fosters trust and encourages employee participation in wellness initiatives. Leaders who prioritize well-being and align organizational goals with employee needs create an environment where wellness programs can thrive. This politicians, organisations, and researchers of the future may benefit from the practical suggestions offered by research. To guarantee that everyone has fair access to healthcare, lawmakers should establish uniform wellness rules that incentivise public-private partnerships. wellness programs for all employees. Organizations are advised to adopt a holistic perspective vi on health, which incorporates not just the physiological but also the psychological, social, spiritual, and ecological aspects of one's well-being. If wellness programs are to have the desired impact on a wide range of employees, they must be designed with their unique requirements in mind. Furthermore, the study emphasises the need of conducting longitudinal studies to evaluate the lasting effects of wellness programs in the workplace. programs and explore other dimensions of well-being, such as financial and career-related factors. Finally, this research highlights how wellness initiatives in the workplace have the power to drastically improve healthcare workers' health, happiness, and productivity. By addressing multiple dimensions of well-being and leveraging technology, organizations can create supportive environments that foster resilience, engagement, and fulfillment among their workforce. The insights gained from This study provides a solid basis for creating wellness initiatives that align with organizational objectives and contribute to sustainable growth.
