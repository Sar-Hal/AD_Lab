Title: Impact of employer branding and organization attractiveness on citizenship behaviour for retention of faculties: a study on faculties of universities of Odisha
Authors: Mishra, Yashaswini
Issue Date: 2019
Abstract: In the era of knowledge competition, human capital has been one of the key parameter to achieve competitive success for an educational institution. Today the contribution of employees is valued as highly significant in promoting and creating an institutional brand in the competitive environment where it is more acute in knowledge based organization such as universities (Feldwick, 1991). Educational institutions are the most crucial organizations in the society for creating most important outcome i.e. human beings with intellectual thought and abreast knowledge. So in today’s parlance individual behaviours in the work culture has been considered more emerging research by experts of organization behaviour and management literatures. Now a days with the challenges posed by globalization across the globe and with mushroom growth of universities, there is massive competition prevails among the educational institutions. On the other hand potential faculties and new aspiring candidates are becoming less attracted, de motivated and less committed to adopt the challenges to improve their performance due to lack of attractive work environment, poor institutional culture, less integrity and respect, lack of effort recognition. In this regard it is a growing concern for research on examining and exploring the role and importance of employer branding and attractiveness factor which will bring positive increased commitment level influenced by citizenship behaviour towards improvement of effectiveness in educational culture. Most of studies have focused on classifying behaviours and emerging reasons that investigate human behaviour at work place like issues of job satisfaction, perception, motivation, occupational attitudes and organization commitment. But the issue which has been emerged by behaviourist and psychologist in recent two decades is OCB. So to keep efficient employees, organizations adopt brand management strategies for an employee, which has given a new light by the advent of employer branding and employer attractiveness concept to the organizational literature. As there is increased competition among universities to produce academic excellence with better skills and competencies, it is essential to create attractive environment, committed faculties for students. In the present scenario the focus of educational institutions is to attain long-term sustainability in all aspects both academic and non-academic. An employer branding and attractiveness dimension plays a significant role in enhancing sense of ownership, sense of accountability and brand name of institutions for educational excellence. So to develop commitment, sense of ownership, satisfaction, engagement level faculties it is needed to align their values with organization values through development of organization citizenship behaviour concept among educational institutions. Researchers have researched and analysed on facets of citizenship behaviour, impact of commitment and job satisfaction on OCB, role of employer branding in talent attraction and retention. This study has tried to find out the influence of different dimensions employer branding and attractiveness on development of citizenship behaviour for retention of faculties. This research work has also attempted to introspect in to implementation of branding factors and citizenship behaviour relationship through regression models. The focus of this research paper is to analyse the influence of employer branding and organization attractiveness factors on organization citizenship behaviour for retention of faculties in Odisha universities covering higher education context. Data were collected from 350 faculties working in different universities of Odisha. Structured questionnaire were used to gather the data for the study. This research has been initiated with introduction to the research followed by intensive review. The conceptual framework was presented. Research methodology was approached containing primary method for data collection with formulation of hypothesis. This research studies the impact of employer branding and attractiveness dimensions on citizenship behaviour pertaining to Odisha university faculties. As per research objectives, ten hypotheses have been verified through correlation and regression analysis tools to analyse the relationship and predictor of citizenship behaviour of faculties like promoting goodwill of institution, less resistance attitude towards changes, helping behaviour of faculties, involvement and sincerity in attendance for organization development, obeying rules even when not watched by organisation. The testing of hypothesis and perception of faculties on factors of employer branding, attractiveness and citizenship behaviour in their institution was carried out by use of statistical techniques like descriptive statistics, Pearson correlation and regression models with the help of structured questionnaire for conducting survey. The results of the research work revealed that citizenship behaviour is significantly predicted and impacted by branding and attractiveness factors like brand knowledge, brand internalization, brand equity, institutional culture and recognition of effort, work place fun, attractive place of employment, respectful environment, sense of belongingness and found to be positive predictor of citizenship behaviour which also shown strong influence on OCB. From the perspective of determination of relationship of employer branding with citizenship behaviour and attractiveness with citizenship behaviour, factors like mentoring culture, career development opportunities, motivation to faculties, brand value importance, respectful environment, team based culture are studied. The findings from the data analysis were interpreted and concluded with the presentation of recommendation limitations, scope for future research, implications and conclusion. The current research work has been undertaken by giving importance to the constructs which entirely highlight on the joint role of employer branding and organization attractiveness on citizenship behaviour for retention university faculties covering private and government universities. However, the scope for future research is not restricted to study of citizenship behaviour of faculties only; it can be extended to other aspects also like administrative staffs of educational institutions starting from principal level to clerical position. Furthermore, future studies and research can be carried out to analyse determining factors of enhancing citizenship behaviour with role of employer branding models in the corporate sector, industrial organization and Government technical universities towards their own work field. The research can be carried in other areas, i.e. student’s perception towards employer branding and attractiveness of educational institution in selection of their career to this teaching profession, effect of attractiveness dimensions on citizenship behaviour of both government and private school teachers and principals, study on retention of younger potential employees covering citizenship behaviour through employer attractiveness. Future research need to focus more on analysis of contribution of other influential and significant variables of organization citizenship behaviour like leadership, engagement, internal marketing, professional commitment, motivation, psychological contract. Result of the study draws a unique attention for educationist and policy makers of higher education to emphasize on increased awareness for branding strategy, employer attractiveness importance and promotion of concept of citizenship behaviour for achievement of sustainability in competitive era and implementations of it among universities and higher educational institutions in true sense. As Organization citizenship behaviour is the increasingly desirable form of employee’s behaviour where employer branding and attractiveness of institution must play a significant role. These concepts should be treated as an effective technological tool in the hand of management and must be adopted strongly in higher educational institutions as well as in other sectors to attract and retain potential employees.
