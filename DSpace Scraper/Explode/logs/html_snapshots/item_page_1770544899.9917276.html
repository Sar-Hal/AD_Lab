Title: STUDY OF THE EFFECTIVENESS OF TECHNOLOGY BUSINESS INCUBATOR SUCCESS FACTORS
Authors: NAYAK, PRIYABRATA
Issue Date: Oct-2023
Publisher: KIIT Deemed to be University
Abstract: The rapid changes in the use of Information and Communications Technologies (ICT) has led to new opportunities and enormous challenges for small and medium scale enterprises and for new venture creation in international trade regimes. Technology Business Incubators (TBIs) ought to be seen as one support mechanism that can complement other tools in the process of linking knowledge organizations for instance technical universities and research institutes to the business sector, promoting new enterprise formation and economic development. Essentially, the TBI is domain controlled by a small management staff. It creates the physical space with shared facilities, counselling, training and information specific to selected technology. It also provides access to all type of university research, financial resources and technical support services, in one integrated and affordable package. Such ‘caring and sharing’ facility leads to business start-ups by reducing initial costs and delays, and to diminish the chances of failure at a fledgling enterprise. Most incubators in the developing countries of Asia and Latin America have a technology focus. On the other hand, in developed countries, less than one-third technology ventures, and mixed-client incubators predominate. Incubators, in general, present seeming contradictions: • The incubator has elements of public good and private gain. • The management team support the external clients and may ‘crowd out’ external services. • Clients may compete and also be benefited by cooperation. But they leave when become successful. • At initial period state support is essential. The analytical research was found to be more appropriate for this study to explore the effectiveness of the TBI eco-system. The objective of this research is to gain a preliminary knowledge about a problem and generate new ideas that will help to explore deeper into the problem (Kotler and Armstrong, 2010). The present study includes a mix of qualitative and quantitative research approach and involves collecting data that have a major role in predicting the possible relationship between the variables/factors of the study. Linear regression analysis model is employed for exploring primary data efficiently. Regression analysis is a statistical process for establishing connections between certain variables. For example, we have one dependent variable and we want to determine how much other independent variables affect it. That is how we get a model of interdependence, and we can use it to predict the dependent variable’s value. Linear regression is an important tool in analytics. The technique uses statistical calculations to plot a trend line in a set of data points. The trend line could be anything from the number of people diagnosed with skin cancer to the financial performance of a company. Linear regression shows a relationship between an independent variable and a dependent variable being studied. There are a number of ways to calculate linear regression. One of the most common is the ordinary least-squares method, which estimates unknown variables in the data, which visually turns into the sum of the vertical distances between the data points and the trend line. The calculations to perform linear regressions can be quite complex. Fortunately, linear regression models are included in most major calculations packages, such as Excel, R, MATLAB and Mathematica. The data analysis of Primary data with various statistical methods and models is correlated. The vital factors of both the TBIs studied have confirmed to the fact that the parameters correspond to their realm expertise. Contribution in the Incubation system and perception by stack holders vary as per quality of supply of inputs and available resources. Evaluation methods are evolving, by focusing on the quality improvisation and on the factors that impact on the effectiveness of TBIs. Research in context with projects, consulting, publications and patents etc. are also related to the programs delivered in the Incubators. The results revealed that most of the respondents have confirmed that value is been added during their course of association in the TBIs. The research has attempted to rationalize its research objective defined for this study and shall contribute to the domain of new knowledge and policy implications.
