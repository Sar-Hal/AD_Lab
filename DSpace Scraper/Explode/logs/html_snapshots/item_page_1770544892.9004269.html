Title: Leadership linkage with internal efficiency of primary schools in gamo gofa zone, ethiopia
Authors: Ambaw, Betta Tsemato
Keywords: Leadership
primary schools
gamo gofa zone
ethiopia
Issue Date: 2019
Abstract: Primary schools’ internal inefficiency was a challenge to education goal achievement in Gamo Gofa Zone whereby huge amount of resource was devoted to re-educate students in previous grades year to year. The achievement of targets set in response to the problem as to how to minimize internal inefficiency was left far behind the standard. As leadership had reflective relationship with organizational performance, this study was designed in order to investigate linkage between the principals’ leadership practice and the schools’ internal efficiency. It was conducted employing quantitative method. Data were collected from principals, teachers and students using descriptive survey questionnaires. They were analyzed using SPSS version 20 through frequency distribution, correlation and multiple regressions models. The study result revealed that all transformational leadership dimensions and contingent reward dimension of transactional leadership had positive correlation, whereas active management by exception, passive management by exception and laissez-faire leadership dimensions had no correlation with the primary schools’ internal efficiency. The multiple regression models indicated that 29 per cent of the variance in the primary schools’ internal efficiency was due to the combined effect of leadership dimensions, and individualized consideration, intellectual stimulation and idealized behavior were the most influential transformational leadership dimensions in predicting the primary schools’ internal efficiency. Yet, these dimensions were practice less than the norm, whereas active management by exception and contingent reward dimensions of transactional leadership were prevailing. The fact that the principals failed to adequately employ full range leadership models contributed to the deterioration of internal efficiency in the primary schools of Gomo Gafa Zone. Thus, it was recommended that the primary school principals need to use full range leadership models, while de-emphasizing the use of active management by exception, passive management by exception and laissez-faire leadership dimensions unless they appear to be important on the basis of situational factors. Moreover, further studies need be conducted in the problem area for excelled and diversified intervention in resolving the problem.
