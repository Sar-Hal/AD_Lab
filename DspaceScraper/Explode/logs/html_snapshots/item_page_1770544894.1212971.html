Title: Improving Efficiency and Quality through Effective Training: An Exploratory Study of Supervisory Leadership Styles in the Infrastructure Sector
Authors: Misra, Pramod Kumar
Issue Date: 2022
Publisher: KIIT Deemed to be University
Abstract: Effective leadership portrays a significant part in any organization’s accomplishments. The leadership position is illustrated whenever people operate in communities and is the central instrument by which a community area or substance frames and decides its needs and culture. By definition, modern initiative is a system of control, which includes a few significant capabilities like enabling, directing, organizing, and empowering the representatives. The rise of authoritative order recommends that initiative isn't really important for the structure flow but its unique value can be developed. Our world is changing continuously and a few decades back, the associations were employing chiefs and they were recruiting pioneers. Today, every supervisor is supposed not exclusively to be a powerful pioneer yet additionally a mentor and mentee. The construction industry is not an exception in this regard, especially concerning the frontline employees, i.e. supervisors and foremen. Supervisors in any construction project are expected to exert a positive influence over the daily performance of their workers. Thus, supervisory leadership is characterized as conduct planned to give direction, support, and coercive input for the day‐to‐day exercises of work unit individuals. The construction sector, which includes metropolitan turn of events and real estate is perceived as India's second largest employer with around 44 million workforces contributing to India's 9% GDP share. Aside, logistics and transportation (i.e. Roads and Highway sectors) contribute to around 15% of the GDP share. This scenario is augmented by the huge impetus for the construction of highway networks in the coming years by GOI. Initiatives include the construction of expressways, coastal and border roads and economic corridors strategized under ‘BharatmalaPariyojana’, which is a jewel scheme of the GOI. By 2022, it is expected that around $82 billion in investments shall be made in this sector during the 1stphase. Furthermore, GOI is aiming to attain an median increase of 7.1% per annum in the construction division, to demonstrate itself as the 3rd global leader in the construction sector. During budget 2018-19, GOI has also allocated US$ 100 billion and allowed 100% FDI investment to boost the infrastructure development. The accessible proof on the business performance isn't empowering despite the fact that the construction area is acquiring conspicuousness in India with the opening-up of Indian business sectors and the beginning of a few mega formative development projects. As per the World Economic Forum report, India has 87th rank in the global v competitiveness index in the infra division. The Ministry of Statistics &Programme Implementation detailed that 40% of the projects, costing around 4.45 million dollars get deferred typically by several months. The Conference of Industry Secretaries report tabled a short-fall in the investment targets in the infrastructure sectors during the 11th five-year plan. Similar alarming issues are additionally obvious in the nations beyond the Indian Domain. Organisation advancement is impacted by phenomenal leadership initiative limits of chiefs or managers alongside reasonable authority style, performance of the employee, job fulfillment, and responsibility towards company objectives and its productive achievement ought to be thought about by all the corporates. Over the couple of years, a project driven paradigm alongside imaginative expertise of technical projected as significant qualities of Effective Construction Supervisory Leadership (ECSL). It is liable for an expansive assortment of designing methods and managerial exercises for any construction project (CP). The opportune effective achievement of the entire venture alongside its appropriate human captial distribution is vigorously subject to ECSL abilities, which renders ECSL as CP's essential leader. There are a plenty of significant jobs of a fruitful ECSL, for example subordinates/subcontractor strengthening, commitment, direction, organized training, constructive criticism, educating lateral thinking, and encouragement. This is additionally upgraded with the viable release of the CP administrator's liability. The driving force of prevailing milieu, quality, and skill and their conditions decides the level of ECSL in a typical CP manager. Thus, the successful execution of projects in the construction industry requires site-supervisory leaders including other frontline leaders' versatile managerial and technical expertise. The present research aims to investigate such prevalent problems in the construction industry in India and examine the impact of supervisory leadership qualities. It presents a comprehensive strategy to identify and gauge the organizational performance (i.e. efficacy and work quality of the employees) via Employee Attitudinal Measures (EAM). It conducts a critical performance assessment of employee attitudes via survey, fact analysis, and data analysis. The leadership of site-supervisory i.e. supervisors including foremen is considered the front-line leadership employed in the target company. The work also summarizes the role of Supervisory Leadership Practices (SLP) in enhancing corporate effectiveness. The concepts considered in this research are focused on SLP as independent variables and EAM as dependent variables. Further, each of these two variables has three major pillars with underlying facets, which were vi used to formulate a customized survey questionnaire having close-ended questions. In light of the study reactions got from the objective populace, elucidating measurements of each construct were conducted and thoroughly analyzed. The research also empirically correlates the site-supervisory leadership traits with organizational performance and summarizes its impetus to meet the GOI’s expectations to achieve an aggressive and sustainable growth rate in the construction sector. The overall aim of the thesis is to suggest ways and methods for improving the organizational efficacy along with a yearning for construction best practices. The research is based on the concept of a ‘Black-Box’ design, where the complex inter-relationship between various aspects related to supervisory leadership practices and organizational performance is properly established and thoroughly examined. It is expected that the outcomes of the present work will be useful for the development of leadership qualities in supervisor-level staff in the typical Indian construction industry by refining the leadership skills through effective training interventions and their proper implementation. Authority styles assume a significant part in deciding performance of the organization, particularly transformational and transactional leadership. Both these leadership styles are important indicators of organizational performance. More specifically, transactional leadership is seen as the accomplishment of organizational goals through a social exchange, while transformational leadership promotes the followers to explore situations differently and quickly react to these alternatives. An extensive literature review showed limited research on SLP and EAM practices including a deep-level study on their possible correlations. Additionally, there are blended finding between administration styles and performance of the organization. Hence, the connection between leadership styles and performance of the organization is uncertain and should be further explored. Effective site-supervisory leadership skills in any construction industry can be a stimulus to the final business outcomes. These skills may include both attitudinal and behavioral traits, e.g. morale and organizational commitment, performance and work quality, and workplace inclusiveness and environment. The importance of measuring employee attitudes stalks from the experiences and behavioral outcomes in the workplace. Attitudes of the employee are a representation of their emotional state that characterizes their relationship with the organization. This state of mind of the frontline employees in any construction industry is extremely vital from the point that they ultimately influence organizational performance. In the radiance of the above vii conversations, the current exploration work has been to concentrate on the accompanying perspectives: 1. To study various leadership traits at the supervisory level that should be followed in a typical Indian construction company to improve its overall effectiveness. 2. To examine how supervisory leadership styles impact the employee attitudinal measures, e.g. morale, organizational commitment, performance, and work quality in the Indian construction industry. 3. To investigate deeply the level of influence and direction of the association-ship between Supervisory Leadership Practices (SLPs) and Employee Attitudinal Measures (EAMs). 4. To explore, suggest and implement an effective training program for a construction organization targeting the development of the leadership skills of supervisors in a complex working environment. Effective Supervisory Leadership Practices (SLPs) are central to any construction company, which is chiefly dependent upon the development of strategic and relevant site supervisory skills. The present dissertation considers this as the core of its research and seeks to correlate the SLP with organizational performances measured through Employee Attitudinal Measures (EAMs). The assumption is that there is a visible impact of these leadership skills on the overall performance of the target company and can be measured in terms of employee turnover, sales per employee, market-to-book values, etc. In nutshell, the proposed study aims to take a deep dive into the newly established concept of effective supervisory leadership practices and their impact on the performance of a construction company using a select case in the Indian industry. The SLP best practices (classified as Independent Variables) were bundled into three major pillars, i.e. Transformational Leadership Style (TFLS), Transactional Leadership Style (TSLS), and Passive Avoidant Leadership Style (PALS). Also, three employee attitudes were considered as Dependent variables, i.e. Morale and Organizational Commitment (MOC), Performance and Work Quality (PWQ), and Workplace Inclusiveness and Environment (WIE). EAMs are the determinants of the effects of an effective SLP. The combinations of these independent and dependent variables resulted in a total of nine research hypotheses. Thus, the research hypothesizes that the adoption of effective site supervisory leadership would assist with further developing the general business execution, which is estimated through attitudes of the employee. The present study also attempts to investigate and deliver empirical indication on the impression of these SLPs on EAMs by answering the research question, "How viii supervisory leadership practices impact the employee attitudes measures in a typical Indian construction industry measured via overall performance, efficiency and qualityof- work?”These hypotheses were examined using Hierarchical Multiple Linear Regression (HMLR) analysis to assess the net impacts of each of the independent variables on the dependent variables. These variables were measured using a suitably designed questionnaire, which was appropriately prepared based on the unique facets under consideration. Few standard sets of questionnaires were studied as a part of the literature review, which served as a platform for the design and operationalization of the customized questionnaire used in the present survey. These standard questionnaires were "Organizational Commitment Questionnaire (OCQ)"; "Job Satisfaction Questionnaire (JSQ)" and "Motivational and Retention Factor Questionnaire (MRQ)". Ten questions were framed for each of these employee attitudinal measures which are the modified version of the original questionnaire. The modification was done to accommodate the requirements of the present research. For all practical purposes of the study, statistics were gathered both from primary and secondary sources. Initially, a pilot study was conducted using some basic questionnaires to fine-tune the survey model. Based on the inferences from the pilot study, an extensive survey using a properly designed set of questionnaires having three separate sections was conducted. The first section comprised of basic demographic analysis, the second section consisted of procedure to identify the effective site-supervisory leadership traits, and the final section consisted of the opinion of employees regarding inclusive training initiatives. Participants were informed that there are no correct responses and that they ought to show their decision by the appropriate number (from the scale of 1 to 5). A score of 3 would demonstrate that the individual had blended perspectives about the decision. A higher score demonstrates the degree to which the organization utilizes the SLP best practices and in any case. Firstly, quantitative findings including descriptive statistics for each of the study variables were explored based on the data collected through the survey. This was followed by a Bivariate analysis to test the connections between the review factors viable. The study utilized relationships to test the relationship, as well as hierarchal multiple regression examination to analyze the net impact of every supervisory leadership practices (SLP) packs on the employee attitudinal measures (EAM) groups. The study revealed that the correlation coefficients between each bundle of SLP and EAM. All factual examinations were performed through Statistical Package for the Social Sciences (SPSS). Primary analysis of the statistics examinations comprised of ix descriptive statistics for all study factors, as well as Cronbach's alpha internal consistency reliability coefficients for the employee questionnaire sub-scales. Then, speculations were tried utilizing stepwise hierarchal multiple regression to gauge the net impact of each and every autonomous factors or the independent variables. The research revealed that transactional leadership style had a negative influence on one of the EAM, i.e. performance and work quality, whereas, transformational leadership style (TFLS) had a positive influence on all the three bundles of EAM. It was also revealed that supervisory leadership practices like supportive leadership, contingent reward, and management by exception are widely being observed in the existing organization, whereas the practice of Laissez-Faire leadership skills is the least. There were a few neutral responses also like inspirational communication, articulating a vision, personal recognition and intellectual stimulation. These responses need to be explored more and associated top practices need to be reestablished within the organization. It was concluded that the organizations ought to focus harder on promotion of the employee, equal job responsibilities and career growth. Pioneers ought to comprehend whether their administration style is emphatically or adversely influencing the employees. The higherlevel management needs to develop or train the supervisors in transformational leadership abilities inside the organisation and the utilization of these styles should connect with the employees' concerns and values. The pioneers ought to support and spur their subordinates not exclusively to perform outstandingly however ensure job satisfaction and commitment towards organization. A significant level of employee attitudinal measure (EAM) will increase employee performance which leads to organizational success. Thus, it can be observed a wide scope for improvement which needs regular training, coaching, and mentoring for the nourishment of favorable employee attitude towards the organization. This will be helpful for industry echelons to minimize non-value adding activities, enhancing employee efficacy beyond 40% along with effective sustainable practices.
