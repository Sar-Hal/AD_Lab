Title: FACTORS AFFECTING THE IMPLEMENTATION OF CRITICAL CHAIN PROJECT MANAGEMENT (CCPM)
Authors: Jena, Bimal Krushna
Issue Date: 2024
Publisher: KIIT Deemed to be University
Abstract: ABSTRACT This study delves into the nuanced world of Critical Chain Project Management (CCPM) implementation within the dynamic context of university infrastructure projects and event management. By examining the intricate web of factors affecting CCPM implementation, this research endeavours to unravel the interplay between Theory of Constraints (TOC) principles and the academic project landscape. Through the comprehensive exploration of ten hypotheses, this study sheds light on the drivers and barriers that shape the successful assimilation of CCPM, enriching both theoretical discourse and practical application. The findings of this study illuminate the paramount importance of top management support as a linchpin for effective CCPM implementation. The resonance between high-level endorsement and project success underscores the strategic role that leaders play in guiding projects toward their objectives. The study also underscores the pivotal role of client consultation, as it emerges as a bridge; linking project teams with stakeholder expectations. Effective communication and collaboration foster a shared vision, minimizing discrepancies and steering projects toward smoother execution. The study's revelations extend to the realm of project planning, where realistic cost and time estimates emerge as essential elements for CCPM integration. The accuracy of initial estimations forms the bedrock upon which efficient resource allocation and scheduling are built. The study also emphasizes the instrumental role of project risk management, where a proactive approach to risk identification, assessment, and mitigation fortifies project resilience and adaptability, crucial attributes in the face of uncertainties. Resource allocation, coupled with a sense of project ownership, stands out as a cornerstone for CCPM success. Adequate provisioning of resources ensures a conducive project environment, optimizing the application of CCPM principles. Simultaneously, fostering project ownership nurtures commitment and responsibility among project team members, igniting a collective drive toward project triumph. The study champions the significance of a shared project mission or common goal, demonstrating how a unified sense of purpose cultivates cohesion, guides decision-making, and aligns projects with CCPM principles. vii While the study's statistical analysis illuminates positive associations between these factors and CCPM implementation, it underscores the intricacies embedded within. The varying degrees of statistical significance reflect the contextual nuances that shape the relationships, influenced by the unique variables inherent to university environments. Moreover, the study extends the application of TOC principles to an uncharted territory, enriching the adaptability and universality of TOC as a framework for addressing complex project scenarios. However, this study is not exempted from limitations. Its concentration on the academic realm may limit its generalizability to other industries. The sample size and self-reported data introduce potential biases, and the study's cross-sectional design precludes establishing causal relationships. The absence of statistical significance for certain factors, such as technical task & their inter-dependencies and project monitoring, underscores the multifaceted nature of CCPM integration. Theoretical contributions abound as this study expands the TOC framework's application, translating its principles into actionable strategies for university project management. Furthermore, its findings resonate practically, guiding project managers, administrators, and stakeholders toward informed decisions that enhance project outcomes. By acknowledging the pivotal role of top management support, client consultation, accurate estimations, risk management, resource allocation, project ownership, and shared project mission, universities can cultivate a culture of effective project execution. In essence, this study is a compass guiding the intricate voyage of CCPM integration within the academic landscape. By weaving together TOC principles and university project dynamics, this research exemplifies the fusion of theory and practice, offering a roadmap for optimizing project management practices. As universities endeavour to navigate the complexities of contemporary project landscapes, this study's findings stand as a guiding light, illuminating pathways toward successful project realization and catalyzing the advancement of project management practices within academia and beyond.
