Title: Study of Shelf Life of Vitamins & Minerals Salts, and Specification & Mapping according to the FCC
Authors: SHRIVASTA, SHARMISTHA
Keywords: Vitamins, minerals salt
Issue Date: 29-Apr-2024
Publisher: KIIT UNIVERSITY
Abstract: Study of Shelf Life of Vitamins & Minerals Salts, and Specification & Mapping according to the FCCf. PROBLEM: Vitamins, minerals salt are essential dietary components, but their potency can degrade over time. This degradation impacts the quality and effectiveness of fortified foods and dietary supplements. Additionally, the specification and mapping of these micronutrients according to the Food Chemicals Codex (FCC) are imperative for maintaining product quality and regulatory compliance,However, the dynamic nature of vitamins and minerals, coupled with the complexity of regulatory standards, presents challenges in accurately determining their shelf life and adhering to FCC specifications. OBJECTIVES: This study aimed to achieve two key objectives. First, it investigated the shelf life of various vitamins & minerals salts, identifying factors that influence their stability. Second, it mapped these micronutrients according to the FCC specifications, providing a reference for our company’s R&D Team. METHADOLOGY:The study employed a combination of literature review and laboratory analysis,it employed a multi-faceted approach to tackle the outlined objectives. Existing research on vitamin & minerals degradation was reviewed to understand the impact of factors like light, temperature, relative humidity.Additionally, laboratory experiments for shelf life study under controlled conditions were conducted using accelerated stability testing methods to simulate long-term storage conditions and assess the degradation kinetics of vitamins and minerals. Analytical techniques such as high-performance liquid chromatography (HPLC) were employed to quantify changes in nutrient concentrations over time. For the FCC mapping, the study analyzed relevant FCC monographs to identify specifications for identification, purity, strength, and storage of vitamins & minerals. ACHIEVEMENTS: The study successfully identified factors affecting the shelf life of vitamins & minerals, shedding light on their stability profiles and factors influencing degradation kinetics. This finding could contribute to a better understanding of the optimal storage conditions and formulation strategies to extend the shelf life of these essential nutrients.Furthermore, our comprehensive analysis of FCC specifications provides valuable insights into the regulatory framework governing the identification, purity, and quality of vitamins and minerals in food products.The developed FCC mapping serves as a reference tool for company’s R&D Team to develop products , ensuring they select and use FCC-compliant ingredients that meet quality and purity standards. This promotes the production of safe and effective food products with consistent vitamin & mineral content.
